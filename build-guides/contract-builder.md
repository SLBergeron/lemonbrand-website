# Contract Builder

You need a service agreement. You could pay a lawyer $500, use some sketchy template from Google, or keep winging it with nothing in writing. None of these are great.

No database. Terms in, contract out. (Disclaimer: not legal advice, always have a lawyer review for real deals.)

The audience is freelancers and consultants who need basic service agreements but don't have legal budgets. The problem is that no contract means no protectionâ€”and bad contracts are sometimes worse than none.

Input: service description, deliverables, timeline, payment terms, what happens if things go wrong (revisions, cancellations, disputes), any specific clauses you need (IP ownership, confidentiality, non-compete). Output: a structured contract with proper legal-ish language.

The model generates from templates and best practices. It covers the standard sections: parties, scope, payment, timeline, IP, confidentiality, termination, limitation of liability, dispute resolution. You customize, you review, you send.

One prompt that understands contract structure. Give it your terms, have it output a document that looks professional and covers the bases.

Demo: describe a simple project with basic terms. Run it. Show the contract with proper sections. "This isn't a substitute for a lawyer on big deals, but for a $3K project? This gives you protection without the overhead."

Hook: handshake deals work until they don't. This gives you a starting point for getting things in writing.
